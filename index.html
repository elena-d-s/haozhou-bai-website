Kung-Fu Forms for Fitness and Performance

Kung-Fu Forms are a great way to stay active, fit and to constantly learn new things. I run a Kung-Fu Fitness class that is perfect for beginners and for anyone that wants to stay fit while practicing Kung-Fu.
I also teach forms for performance and high level training. For those interested in learning traditional Chinese Kung-Fu, I am teaching a variety of styles that I learned during my training: Kung-Fu Weapons (22 forms), Kung-Fu Animals style (12 forms), Shaolin Kung-Fu (18 forms), Baji Fist style (3 forms), Bagua Fist style (4 forms), Traditional Fist style (5 forms), Drunken Fist and Weapon style (3 forms), Modern Wushu Fist and Weapons (5 forms).
As part of the Kung-Fu Forms classes you will also learn acrobatics, from beginner level to advanced: cartwheel, aerial kick, butterfly kick and butterfly twist, tornado kick, splits, flips, and so on.

Sparring and Self-Defence

Basic self-defense skills are useful for everyone. I run a beginner self-defense class that everybody can join to learn blocking, release from hold, basic punch and kick.
For more advanced training, I teach sparring, where you can learn high level self-defense and combat skills for Sport Competitions.

Tai-Chi and Qi-Gong for Health and Wellness

One of the most important lessons I learned in my training was that wellness and the ability to develop calmness are the foundations of Kung-Fu practice. This is why I run a Tai-Chi class and a Qi-Gong Wellness class that you can take to complement your Kung-Fu Training.
If you are only interested in learning Tai-Chi and Qi-Gong Wellness on their own, you can practice 13 Qi-Gong Wellness forms and learn Sun, Chen and freestyle Tai Chi.
